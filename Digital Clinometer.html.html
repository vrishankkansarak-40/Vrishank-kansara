Core Trigonometry Formula

Total Object Height Calculation:

$H = E + D \cdot \tan(\theta)$

This formula combines your **eye height** ($E$) with the calculated height above or below your eye level ($D \cdot \tan(\theta)$) to find the object's total height from the ground ($H$).

Variable Key

  • $H$: Total Object Height.
  • $E$: Your measured Eye Height.
  • $D$: Distance from you to the object.
  • $\theta$: The measured Angle of elevation or depression.
  • *Note: The angle $\theta$ is automatically converted from degrees to radians for the $\tan$ function in the calculation.
